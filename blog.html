What is carbon emission?

Carbon Emission means the release of greenhouse gases and/or their precursors into the atmosphere over a specified area and period of time. Carbon dioxide emissions or CO2 emissions are emissions stemming from the burning of fossil fuels and the manufacture of cement; they include carbon dioxide produced during consumption of solid, liquid, and gas fuels as well as gas flaring.

How carbon emission affect the world

The greenhouse effect is supported by science and data, despite the fact that it is a current global hot topic. Like a genuine greenhouse, the greenhouse effect traps gases from the Earth's atmosphere in the warmth of the sun. The combustion of fossil fuels is the main cause of the increase in greenhouse gas emissions during the past 150 years, which is primarily attributable to human activity, according to the EPA. As discussed, carbon dioxide is one of the biggest contributors to the changes in our atmosphere and greenhouse gases overall.
